If the idea is not clear in your mind, English will feel much harder because your brain is trying to do two things at the same time: create the idea and find the English words.

In that situation, don’t force yourself to speak English immediately. Try this:

First, pause and organize your idea in a very simple way.

Ask yourself:  
“What is my main point?”  
“Why do I think this?”  
“Can I give an example?”

Then say a simple English version.

For example, you don’t know how to explain your second brain idea. Start with:

“I have many thoughts.”  
“I don’t want to lose them.”  
“I want to connect them.”  
“So I built this system.”

That is enough. You can add details later.

Also, you can use “thinking phrases” when you need time:

“Let me think for a second.”  
“What I mean is…”  
“The main idea is…”  
“I’m trying to say that…”

These phrases give your brain a little space to build the next sentence.
